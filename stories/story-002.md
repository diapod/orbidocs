# Story 002: Federated Peer Learning and Consensus Correction

## Sequence of Steps

1. A node connects to an IRC-like network and joins one of the public thematic channels.
2. The node sends a query.
3. Other nodes respond and enrich the querying node's knowledge.
4. Nodes that detect a significant mismatch between a response and their own knowledge (and suspect the response may be incorrect) may join an ad-hoc channel dedicated to that specific question and answer, share observations, and discuss toward a consensus.
5. The node stores the collected knowledge and uses it to enrich vector memory immediately, or by creating a new file in scanned directories, or by training a mini language model that supports specialization-specific or general knowledge tasks.
